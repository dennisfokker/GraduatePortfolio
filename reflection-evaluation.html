REFLECTION AND EVALUATION

I requested for a peer review two times: once roughly mid-way through my internship and one near the end. A summary document can be found here . This contains summary graphs of all the answers, links to the documents that contain the full answers provided and links to the forms themselves.

A reflection on the received reviews, a personal review and evaluation can be found below.

Reflection peer reviews

The questions are constructed based on previous peer review documents from Fontys and adjusted to be related to Enversed’s workflow.

Simply looking at the summaries of the graphs (even after the transformation suggested in the summary document), a clear increase can be seen. Going from a 4-5 score to a 5-6. Applying the translation, this would form an approximate score going from 4.3 to 4.8 (on the scale provided in the summary document).

Given their feedback, they find me a reliable and social colleague, providing a healthy work environment that helps with getting stuff done quickly, but properly. On the other side I can get a bit nit-picky or stubborn, making certain discussions last longer than they should.

Virtually all the feedback provided in the peer reviews is something that was in-line with how I see myself, or new but something I could agree with.

Personal reflection

A lot of the things I see in the peer reviews are things I would’ve noted myself (such as being very proactive in the team, but occasionally being a bit too firm on my ideas).

Before my internship started, I wrote down three subjects I felt like I could improve upon or should exploit during my internship. They are listed below with my reflection on them.

Communication: discussions can take longer than they should, as I can have issues separating issues of primary importance from those of secondary importance. While it’s still something I need to work on, I do believe it’s going better. It’s something everybody at my previous internship noticed, but it’s only noted as a minor thing during this one. I keep a better view of the total scale of the subject.

Independance: I request for feedback in a timely fashion without being a nuisance or wasting too much time attempting to fix something myself. This was a strength noted during my first internship, and still very well seems to be the case. I keep a good rhythm between when I ask for help and when I think I can fix it myself within a manageable timeframe. I also always provide plenty of information about what the problem is, what my theories are and what I have already tried, providing much faster support.

Perfectionism: Are my products of proper quality, without spending far too much time on components that won’t matter in the end. This was a weakness that has haunted me for most of my studies. This somewhat joins up with the primary/secondary importance issue. I think I provide structured reviews without hammering on too much detail (“letting it slip” or informing informally). I believe this also comes forth in my general pace of work, eventually declaring it “Good enough”, even though I would much rather make it much more extensive (which simply won’t be needed for the current use-case).

Evaluation

The internship started a bit under-par for my personal taste, but this was mostly since I didn’t have an extensive knowledge of Unreal Engine and its networking yet. As the internship progressed my tasks passed reviews much quicker and I could also provide valuable insight on the reviews of other people.

The documentation part started fairly well, working on it a few hours a week, but near the middle I started to slip up until eventually coming to a halt, which quickly came to bite me back near the end.

I believe I performed all my tasks in a structured manner, making the best out of less-optimal situations where previous people left off. I do believe I should have made more calls for refactoring, where the current quality of the codebase would start to become hard to read or expand upon.
