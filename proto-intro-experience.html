INTRO EXPERIENCE

Intro experience is a game about you as an inventor, drawing out all the inventions: the ones you use in the other games.
Trace the guiding line and see the created doodle come to life, into something you’ll get to operate later!

CONCEPT

The introduction experience of PROTO: get you first experience with VR and make sure it’s a simple, easy going game. Hold your pencil and start tracing the predetermined drawings after which they come to life to show the inventions that will be used in the later games.

DEVELOPMENT

The drawing system went through several iterations. Whilst the base system stayed the same, certain changes were made as the experience either wasn’t very fun yet, or not polished enough.

Drawing V1

Drawings are created by shaping one or more splines (order maintained through tags). Upon spawning, it’ll go over all actors, creating point meshes (visuals) at all spline points and a spline mesh for every spline section between two points (since a spline mesh is only from point A to B and doesn’t support intermediate nodes).

When a player touches a spline point (done through a distance check between the spline point and the tip of the pencil), the point is activated (texture animation) and the connected spline mesh is made visible (again, through a texture animation).

Whenever a spline is finished, an animation can be fired and the next spline can be activated, until you finish all splines and thus the drawing is finished, triggering a different animation.

Drawing V2

The previous version created a feeling of “touch the dots”: you just touch a point, look where the next point is and touch that one.

In order to create a more drawing feeling a “hint line” was added. This essentially was another state on the meshes that had a sort of transparent/greyed-out visual. These states were a simple float parameter in the material where some states had transitions (e.g. interpolate from 2.0 to 3.0).

With this players roughly know ahead of time where they’ll need to move towards and can thus make a more continuous movement.

Drawing V3

The points were still very distracting, and no feedback is provided whilst drawing. In fact, it was still a “touch the dots” experience, just with more visuals upon touching a point.

This version actually allowed the player to draw. The points were completely removed, although they were still used in the back-end system. We use the “touched” points to keep track at what section of the player is (and if he has started/ended that specific spline). Furthermore the spline visuals now update live with where your pencil is on the spline: a bright blue line showing what you’ve drawn so far.

Now you were finally drawing the inventions, a line showing up where your pencil passes over the hint line.

Later this was extended with a few extra visuals, but these didn’t change anything noteworthy on a functional level.
